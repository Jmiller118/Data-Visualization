Abortion Rates

Abortion is a hot topic and has been for years. Left side Democrats support abortion rights and hard-core Republicans are against abortion rights. This has been ongoing debate since the landmark decision of Roe v. Wade of 1973. Roe v. Wade gave women the right to chose to have an abortion without restrict government restriction. Today, the argument lies in “my body, my right to choose” to “taking another human’s life”.

Left Chart

The left chart is to support Left Side Democrats that abortion rates have barely increased since 1980. This is demonstrated in a line chart that selects all the data from the original dataset. However, the y-axis is skewed to go to 200. This the simplification techinque that drastically reduces the complexity of the data. This allows the line to appear straight for anyone just glancing at the chart. The color and thickness of the line are also important. The green allows the brain to consider it okay since green is associated with things that are not bad, this is a highlighting techinque. The thickness of the line is thin to make the line appear less dramatic helping the argument that the rate has hardly increased. This is the visual metaphor tricking the brain into thinking green is good and thin means hardly any change.

Right Chart

The right chart is to support Right Side Republicans that abortion rates have increased since Roe v. Wade. This is demonstrated in a line chart as well, but only selects a few years of data. The x-axis data is only read in until read 2005 due to that being the sharpest line increase. This the the omission techinque allowing parts of the data to not be explained. The y-axis is also skewed by starting at eight instead of zero. This is the biasing techique that allows one narrative to be shown over another. Another way this chart supports Republicans is the color and width of the line. Red is to indicate is it bad and should be stopped, the thickness makes it stand out more. It appears worse to the eye than the left chart making the argument stronger for increasing. These are visual metaphors that chose different techinques and use associations to make a point.
